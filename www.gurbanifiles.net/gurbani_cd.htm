INFORMATION  ABOUT  THE  CONTENTS  OF  THE  GURBANI CD

 

A general outline of what is on the Gurbani-CD

The computer data on the Gurbani CD includes:

·    Text of Siri Guru Granth Sahib, Nitnem Banis and Bhai Gurdaas's Banis in Gurmukhi (in different formats).

·    Text of Siri Guru Granth Sahib, Nitnem Banis and Bhai Gurdaas's Banis in Devanagari (in different formats).

·    Translation of the Text of Siri Guru Granth Sahib by Singh Sahib Sant Singh Khalsa, MD (in the customary Siri Guru Granth Sahib format and sentence by sentence Gurmukhi/Devanagari to English translation).

·    Translation of the Text of Siri Guru Granth Sahib in Punjabi (Guru Granth Darpan) by Professor Sahib Singh. The typed text of his is made available by S. Avtar Singh Dhami.

·    Dictionary (Kosh) of Siri Guru Granth Sahib in Punjabi (SGGS Kosh) by Dr. Gurcharan Singh, Ph.D. This is an honest update of all previous dictionaries.

·    MahanKosh encyclopedia by Kahan Singh Nabha, in pdf format. This is made available by Bhai Baljinder Singh.

·    Phonetic transliteration of the Text of Siri Guru Granth Sahib in common English characters in different formats and in customized characters (in customary layout).

·    Many types-faces (fonts): many are variations of Gurmukhi, a few of Devanagari & 1 of GurbaniRomanizing. Unicode Gurmukhi fonts based on the international standard are also included on this version of the Gurbani-CD. Many important documents on this version of the Gurbani-CD use Unicode fonts.

·    A number of databases of Siri Guru Granth Sahib text in different formats including alphabetized databases.

·    A number of valuable documents relating to Sri Nanak Parkash and Sri Gur Partap Granth.  The electronic compiling of these done by Bhai Baljinder Singh.

·    A number of photographs of many of the historic Gurdawaras and of the associated artwork in color.

·    A valuable document  "Maansarovar" compiled by Dr. Kulwant Singh. 

·    Valuable documents by Dr. Gurcharan Singh including "Haumai" and "Nadir". 

·    A useful 263 page long document (from a 291 page book) on Sikh Religion written in English by Sikh Missionary Center, PO BOX 62521, Phoenix, Arizona 85082 (current address). The lives of the ten Sikh Gurus and the basic Sikh philosophy are nicely depicted in this document.

 

The written documents on the Gurbani CD are provided for use with the following programs:

1.      Microsoft Word 2003 or later.

2.      Adobe Acrobat Reader.

Acrobat Reader files often provide the best appearance on the computer monitor.

NOTES:

1.      The documents of Siri Guru Granth Sahib in the customary format have been created in such a way that on most commonly used computer systems a correct number of pages will be displayed (1430 in all). However, on some computers depending upon their printer set-up the displayed number of pages of MS Word documents may be more than the actual. This problem can be easily fixed by reducing the left & right margins of the documents. In rare instances, it may also require a slight adjustment of the paragraph indentation/margins of some of the headline paragraphs. Acrobat Reader files always display proper number of pages because the page numbers of Acrobat Reader files do not change with the change of printer setup.

2.      For the purpose of eliminating typing errors, the Gurbani-CD text has been compared with Siri Guru Granth Sahib Bir published in March 1994 by Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandak Committee, Amritsar. Anyone trying to investigate the spelling accuracy of the text of Siri Guru Granth Sahib given on the Gurbani-CD should only compare the text with the cited publication. The given references regarding page and line numbers in all the documents on the Gurbani-CD tally with the above cited publication.
